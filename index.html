Salvador, officially the Municipality of Salvador (Maranao: Inged a Salvador; Cebuano: Lungsod sa Salvador; Tagalog: Bayan ng Salvador), is a 5th class municipality in the province of Lanao del Norte, Philippines. According to the 2020 census, it has a population of 32,115 people.Salvador was created from Baroy through Executive Order No. 370 signed by President Carlos P. Garcia on January 13, 1960.[5]

Barangays

Salvador is politically subdivided into 25 barangays. Each barangay consists of puroks while some have sitios.



Distances

Based on the great-circle distance (the shortest distance between two points over the surface of the Earth), the cities closest to Salvador are Tangub, Misamis Occidental, Ozamiz, Misamis Occidental, Pagadian, Zamboanga del Sur, Marawi, Lanao del Sur, Iligan, and Oroquieta, Misamis Occidental. The nearest municipalities are Sapad, Lanao del Norte, Kapatagan, Lanao del Norte, Lala, Lanao del Norte, Nunungan, Lanao del Norte, Baroy, Lanao del Norte, and Magsaysay, Lanao del Norte.
